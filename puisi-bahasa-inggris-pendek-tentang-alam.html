Definition of Tsunami

         tsunami

Tsunami is a very large water waves generated by a variety of disturbances in the bottom of the ocean. These disorders may include earthquakes, drift, or volcanic eruption. Tsunami invisible while still being away at sea, but once reach shallow areas, this fast-moving waves will get bigger.

The tsunami also often mistaken as a tidal wave. This is because when it reaches land, it is more like a wave tide higher than usual waves that resemble naturally reach the shore by the wind. But the actual tsunami wave has no relation whatsoever with the tide of events. Therefore, to avoid false understanding, oceanographic experts often use the term seismic sea waves (seismic sea wave) to refer to a tsunami, which is scientifically more accurate.

     Causes of tsunami waves
    Tsunamis can be triggered by a variety of disorders (disturbance) on the large-scale sea water, such as earthquakes, plate shifts, volcanic eruption under the sea, or the collision of celestial bodies. Tsunamis can occur when the seafloor moves suddenly and having a vertical displacement.
 
Steps should be taken Sinoman Disaster- include:

     Map highest tsunami inundation-prone areas, evacuation routes, temporary shelters and safe enough.
     Coordinate with the Meteorology and Geophysics Agency (BMG), the police, local authorities, and hospitals. If the data from BMG regarding disaster early warning can not be expected to speed, this community should collect natural phenomena are not uncommon.
     Conduct regular meetings to increase knowledge about the earthquake and tsunami. If necessary, bring in an expert.
     Doing exercises regularly, both scheduled and unscheduled.
     Create an evacuation response time deadlines to be applied in practice so that in a real catastrophe has been used to respond quickly.
     Make certain codes that are recognized around the community to indicate evacuation. Such as in Simeulue, closest to the epicenter of the earthquake in Aceh, has a term Semong shouted repeatedly to indicate the presence of a tsunami. With this code, automatic evacuation should be done as soon as possible to a more tinggi.Menyebarkan images evacuation maps in remote areas where community members live.
     Deploying images evacuation maps in remote areas where community members live.

While the steps that must be done for each individual is:

     Prepare an emergency bag filled with refuge purposes for 3 days. This includes, clothes, food, securities, and drink in moderation. Do not carry a bag too heavy because it will reduce the agility of mobility.
     Always respond to every workout seriously as the time of the disaster.
     Always sensitive to the unusual natural phenomena.

To read the signs of nature before the tsunami, Amien Widodo give some clues based on previous experience tsunamis.

     A rumble that occurs due to shifting soil layer. This sound can be heard for hundreds of miles as it did when the earthquake and tsunami in Pangandaran ago.
     If the epicenter was at a depth below sea level shallow and strength more than 6 on the Richter scale, need to watch a tsunami.
     Sweep period of tsunami waves in coastal epicenter can be calculated based on the distance to the coast.
     Rapidly receding shoreline because of the force created a shift in the soil layer. The recession of the shoreline may be quite far away.
     Because reflux coastline, smell-like odor characteristic fishy smell and sometimes the smell of sulfur.
     For a region that has a network of underground pipes, damage to pipelines caused by ground movement.
     In some cases, the behavior of animals can also be an early warning of a tsunami. Shortly before the tsunami in Aceh, thousands of birds to panic and stay away from the coast, while the elephants in Thailand restless and also away from the beach.
